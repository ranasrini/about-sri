Written by Rana Srinivasan about Development on July 12, 2018

Web developers are typically specialized to develop for one of two roles: the front-end—the dynamic, interactive portion of a website that everyone is familiar with—and, the back-end—the data-driven server-side of an application that powers site functionality from behind the scenes. The skills you’ll be looking for in a web developer will differ depending on which role you are trying to fill.

Another important aspect of your project you’ll want to define is project duration or time to completion. Web development projects can range from weeks to months or more. The length of time is often determined by the developer based on your project description, but it’s generally good practice to provide an estimate or a deadline, if applicable.

MY WEBPAGE

Written by Rana Srinivasan in Development on July 20, 2018

Using a combination of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, a front-end web developer builds everything a user sees and experiences on a website. They take storyboards, UI/UX mockups, and abstract design concepts and turn them into functioning front-end features like dynamic embedded elements, sliders, drop-down menus, and call to action buttons. Everything from the manner in which web content like articles and videos are displayed to design choices like fonts and color schemes, to the overall layout of a web page must be implemented by a front-end developer.

Web developers are as diverse as the technologies they wield. Clearly defining your project requirements can help you narrow your field of potential applicants by targeting those who possess the specific tools and skills you need to bring your web project to life.
